Everything you need to know about peptides, reconstitution, and safe handling.

What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the same building blocks as proteins, but smaller. Research peptides are synthetic versions of naturally occurring peptides your body already produces. They interact with specific receptors to drive targeted biological effects, which is why they've become a focal point in performance, recovery, and longevity research.

What Is Reconstitution?

Peptides are sold as lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder in vials. Before use, you dissolve the powder by adding bacteriostatic water (BAC water) — this is called reconstitution. The amount of water you add determines the concentration, which determines how many units you draw per dose. This calculator does that math for you instantly.

How the Calculator Works

For a single-compound vial: enter the mg in the vial, BAC water added, and desired dose — get units to draw. For blended vials: enter units you plan to draw and see the exact dose of each compound you'll receive. Because blends are one solution, you can't dose each compound independently — the ratio is fixed by the reconstitution.

Units vs. mL

A U-100 insulin syringe has 100 units per mL. When the calculator says "draw 20 units," that means 0.20 mL of solution. Most people work in units because the markings on insulin syringes are easier to read precisely. Common syringe sizes: 1 mL (100 units), 0.5 mL (50 units), 0.3 mL (30 units).

Storage and Handling

Store unreconstituted vials in the refrigerator, away from light. Once reconstituted with BAC water, keep refrigerated and use within 28–30 days. Never freeze a reconstituted vial. Keep vials upright. If you need to mix, gently swirl — never shake. Always use sterile technique: new syringe per injection, alcohol-swab the stopper.

Injection Basics

Most research peptides are administered subcutaneously (sub-Q) — a shallow injection into the fat layer, typically the lower abdomen. Use a fresh insulin syringe for every injection. Swab the vial stopper with alcohol before drawing. After filling the syringe, tap to bring air bubbles to the top and gently depress the plunger to clear them.
